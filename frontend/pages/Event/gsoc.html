Google Summer of Code

Global program for advanced contributors

Google Summer of Code (GSoC) is a highly prestigious, 12+ week online program where you are paid a stipend to contribute to open-source organizations. Since it is currently late December 2025, you are in the perfect "pre-application" window to prepare for GSoC 2026.

Official GSoC Portal

Your GSoC Journey

1

Find Organization

February 19, 2026 onwards

Google announces the official list of mentoring organizations. Shortlist 2-3 organizations that match your tech stack.

2

Write Proposal

March 16 - March 31, 2026

Draft your proposal early and share it with mentors for feedback. Include technical breakdown, timeline, and your experience.

3

Coding Period

May 25 onwards

If accepted (announced April 30), begin coding for 12-22 weeks. Pass mid-term and final evaluations to receive stipend payments.

4

Community Bonding

May 1-24, 2026

Set up your development environment, meet your mentor, and finalize project milestones before coding begins.

Program Phases

Success in GSoC is 80% preparation. Here's your roadmap to success:

1

Preparation

December – January

Build your "proof of work" before organizations are announced.

Show Key Actions
  • Master the Tools: Get proficient with Git and GitHub (forking, branching, PRs)
  • Build Your Profile: Make contributions to open-source projects with "good first issue" labels
  • Identify Your Tech Stack: Choose your focus area (Web, Systems, AI/ML, Mobile)
2

Organization Selection

February 19, 2026 onwards

Google announces mentoring organizations. Time to shortlist and engage.

Show Key Actions
  • Shortlist 2-3 Organizations: Filter by your tech stack on GSoC website
  • Join the Community: Find their Discord/Slack and introduce yourself professionally
  • Study the Idea List: Review project proposals and pick ones matching your skills
3

Proposal Period

March 16 – March 31, 2026

Application phase. Submit up to three well-crafted proposals.

Show Key Actions
  • Draft Early: Share drafts with mentors for feedback
  • Synopsis: 50-word project summary
  • Technical Breakdown: Detailed implementation approach
  • Timeline: Week-by-week coding plan
  • Bio: Your experience and contributions
4

The Program

May – August 2026

Accepted contributors begin coding and complete evaluations.

Show Key Actions
  • Community Bonding: May 1-24 - Setup and planning
  • Coding Period: May 25 onwards - 12-22 weeks of development
  • Evaluations: Pass mid-term and final evaluations for stipend
